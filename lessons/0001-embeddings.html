Day 8/30 — Embeddings

Vector Embeddings and Semantic Search

How representing text as coordinates in space allows computers to search by concept rather than keywords.

1. What is an Embedding?

A computer cannot read meaning, but it excels at geometry. An embedding is a process that translates text (words, sentences, or code chunks) into a list of numbers called a vector. This vector represents coordinates in a high-dimensional space.

If two pieces of text share a similar meaning, their coordinates will be close to each other. By computing the angle or distance between these vectors, we perform semantic search.

Key Idea: "Feline" and "Cat" share no characters, but in embedding space, their vector angles point in almost the same direction.

3. The Mathematics of Cosine Similarity

To find how close two vectors $A$ and $B$ are, we calculate their Cosine Similarity. This measures the cosine of the angle between them, returning a score between $-1$ (opposite direction) and $1$ (same direction):

Cosine Similarity = (A • B) / (||A|| * ||B||)
  • Dot Product (A • B): Multiply matching coordinates and sum them: (A[0]*B[0]) + (A[1]*B[1]) + ...
  • Magnitude (||A||): The length of the vector, calculated using the Pythagorean theorem: sqrt(sum(coords^2))
